Considerations for Selectng from H.S.M.'s

Read the guidelines! If you assume they're identical to insurance (except on a religious framework), disappointments (& maybe even financial-disaster) reaching you may prove otherwise. Also, treatment of pre-existing conditions will vary.

Considering the religious nature of (genuine, good-faith) H.C.S.M.'s (and given the ministries' target-audience are mostly practicing Christians), medical-expenses from violations of generally-accepted Christian morals (ex. from committing illegal activity, drug-abuse, intoxication, etc.)‚Ä¶ are not likely to be considered. Abortions, sterilization, contraception, and the like are also unlikely to be considered. (For specifics on each ministry, read the guidelines.) Some require regular church-attendance, with a minister's affidavit to certify this. (Personally, I know L.H.S. does not. Also, check the others, for more options.) Requiring members to live by conservative values (ex. avoiding substance-abuse, promiscuity, etc.) also serves serves another purpose: lowering medical-expenses (ex. avoiding expenses from chronic drug-abuse on the body, sexually-transmitted diseases, etc.).

As of S.P.P. book-edition's wwriting (back in 2014), NONE of the 4 book-listed ministries ever failed to pay an eligible bill (back when H.S.M.'s were little-known and the memberships were mostly healthy and/or health-conscious). As can be seen from online-reviews after that year, the masive influx of the rest of the population when it became more popularized ended this. For certain, different H.C.S.M.'s have been coping, adjusting, and trying to recover from this. (More on that below.)

Caveat on H.C.S.M.'s, in General:

ALSO: DO YOUR RESEARCH with H.S.M.'s! Some have gotten into legal-trouble, even getting banned from operating in certain States.
(Also, full disclosure: I've personally been a L.H.S.-member for a decade now.)

Now that the caveats have been mentioned, how do H.S.M.'s work?

  • Since they're legally NOT INSURANCE, insurance-terms are not be used with them. Hence, each ministry has its own terminology. For example, premiums are't collected by L.H.S.: "monthly contrubutions" are. Medical expenses are "shared", not covered. The said ministry has no insurance-plan with any deductables, but an "Annual Unshared Amount (A.U.M.)" does exist with each level of membership offered.
  • The system runs on altruism, mutual-charity, and religious obligaiton, as opposed to legal entitlements.

Has the last point been abused before? You bet! (Recently, bankruptcy was filed by a so-called "Trinity HealthShare" that the Alliance has rightly called a "sham" after being found guilty of fraud.) Be careful who gets awarded your hard-earned money!
